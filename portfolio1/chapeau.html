Chapeau! is a restaurant re-branding and visual design project for a high end French bistro in the Inner Richmond district of San Francisco

problem definition

Chapeau!’s brand identity has not been updated for over 20 years. Their problems are:

  1. They have 2 versions of logos that don’t match. Different typefaces within each logo also don’t go together
  2. Poorly constructed website with a floating footer
  3. Customers have problems reading their paper menu with their dim light
  4. New customers can’t tell what the dishes are by their names

project goals

  1. Design a new visual identity that is modern and meets the owner's expectations
  2. Streamline the navigation and improve the user experience of Chapeau!'s website
  3. Elevate Chapeau!'s brand image and improve it's business

interview with the owner

Philippe is the owner and head chef of Chapeau!. I gained a lot of useful insights from eating at his restaurant and conversing with him. Below is the philosophy of his restaurant and cooking style.


  1. We offer affordable luxury, great food at reasonable prices
  2. We are an upscale bistro with casual dining experience, no dress code
  3. Traditional southern French food with a modern twist
  4. We are always trying to reinvent ourselves
  5. We don’t always stick to recipes. Our chefs add their own creative spins to traditional recipes
Meaning of "Chapeau!"
“In French, chapeau without an exclamation mark means a hat. With an exclamation mark, "chapeau!" means “wow”! It’s a gesture to take off your hat and bow down to show your respect .”
Chef's Personality
“I’m a creative person, I’m also an amateur photographer and actor. I like modern designs that are simple and straightforward”
Cooking Philosophy
”We cook traditional southern French food with a modern flair while adding our own creative spins to traditional recipes”

competitive analysis

Most French restaurants in San Francisco that are in Chapeau!'s price range have rustic and traditional brand identities. I therefore decided to create a new identity for Chapeau! that’s modern and refined to stand out from their competitors.

digital menu

Like most high end restaurants, Chapeau!'s menu doesn't have pictures. Most Americans don't understand French and have problems ordering their food. The lighting of their restaurant is dim. Older customers have problem reading texts in their paper menu. I therefore decided to design a digital menu with images and larger texts to facilitate their customers visualizing and ordering food.

website

Chapeau!'s current website has many navigation issues. There are dead links in the header. The footer floats to the top as you scroll down the page. Some food looks undercooked in the photo gallery. In addition, they have a different versions of their logo that doesn't go with their official logo. This creates a confusing brand image. I decided to redesign the website and clean up the clutter.
